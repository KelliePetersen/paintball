Frequently Asked Questions

What is Paintball?

Paintball is what you get when you combine tag, hide and seek, guns, and paint. There are many variations of the game. Some popular games are Capture the Flag, where two teams attempt to cross terrain and claim the opponent's flag, and deathmatch, where there are no teams and everyone just fights each other. Games vary in time, but most players will get in several games in a session.

Is it War?

Paintball can sound aggressive to some people. In reality, Paintball is a well organized, exciting and expanding sport, where good sportsmanship and friendly behaviour is highly valued. Paintball has been used to improve teamwork, problem solving and camaraderie since the 1980's. People who are completely new to Paintball, or just want a laidback experience, will have a great time at our field.

Is it Dangerous?

Safety is the key word at Lowell Paintball. You must be careful while running in the woods, but the only way a player can be injured by playing paintball is if he or she removes their goggles during play, or if a player uses a gun in an unsafe manner. Our judges are there to make sure everyone follows the safety rules. Any infraction of the safety rules will result in immediate ejection from the field. It is always safety first!

Does it hurt?

That's one of the most commonly asked questions. The answer is if you are wearing sensible clothing like a jacket, thick jumper or overalls ... then no. If you get hit by a pellet at close range on bare skin then expect a sharp shock. It might sting for a minute and leave a small welt at worst.

Who plays Paintball?

Everyone! We've hosted events for many different groups like birthdays, sororities, corporate events, and tournaments. Skill level is not a concern at all, and new players are very welcome. Parent/guardian consent is required for players under 18 years of age.

What equipment do we need?

All equipment can be rented from us. See the Activities page for more information. Each player needs a paintball gun, paint pellets, and a protective mask. Each player is armed with a C02 marker that shoots soft gelatin balls the size of a bath bead. These are filled with a washable paint that splatters upon impact.

Can I bring my own equipment?

Paintball pellets must be purchased from us because of health and safety concerns.

What should I wear?

It's recommended to wear clothing that fully covers your skin. Pants and a long sleeved shirt would be ideal. Thicker clothing like jackets or overalls may also help soften the blow of a paintball pellet.

Will paintball ruin my clothes?

The paint contained in the balls are a washable paint. That said, it isn't recommended to wear your best clothing. Running around the field may rip or dirty your clothes.
